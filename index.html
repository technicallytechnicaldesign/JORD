About

A few soft voices, generated live — no recordings. The hum leans warmer at night, brighter by day, and stirs a little when something drifts by to visit. Swell decides whether the whole thing breathes — holding one steady level, or rising and falling in slow, uneven waves. Variety sets how much it wanders — push it and the drone genuinely roams, not just a little. There's an optional soft percussion layer too, with its own volume — pick a texture and let Groove decide how loose it feels; at the vibey end it really lets loose, with syncopation, dropped beats, and the odd texture swap. Dynamics decides how far the hits swing between soft and loud — even at one end, ghost-quiet to nearly double at the other. There's a slower chimes layer too — singing bowls, bells and a deep temple bowl that swell and ring out on their own unhurried clock, sparse and never on a grid. It pauses itself when you leave the tab.
